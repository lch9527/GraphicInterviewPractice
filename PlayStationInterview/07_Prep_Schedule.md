# Prep Schedule

Use this schedule once the HM interview date is known.

## 5 Days Before

- Read `01_HM_45_Min_Runbook.md`.
- Fill in Story 1 and Story 2 in `02_Story_Bank.md`.
- Pick the best graphics/GPU story and the best C++/systems story.
- Review `06_Company_And_Person_Research.md`.

## 4 Days Before

- Study codec basics in `03_Technical_Study_Sheet.md`.
- Practice explaining:
  - I/P/B frames
  - GOP
  - bitrate vs quality vs latency
  - hardware acceleration
  - CPU/GPU synchronization

## 3 Days Before

- Do one 45-minute spoken mock using `04_Mock_Questions.md`.
- Record or time yourself if possible.
- Fix answers that are too long or too vague.

## 2 Days Before

- Drill technical answers:
  - low-latency cloud gaming pipeline
  - frame drop debugging
  - bounded producer-consumer queue
  - GPU readback stall
  - H.264 vs H.265 vs AV1

- Run or review these existing repo exercises:
  - `questions/coderpad_rendering/Q14_Frame_Budget_CPU_GPU_Bottleneck.cpp`
  - `questions/data_structure_builder/Q15_Implement_Circular_Buffer_Queue.cpp`
  - `CUDA practice/Project_04_CUDA_Array_Reduction`

## 1 Day Before

- Rehearse:
  - 30-second intro
  - 2 technical stories
  - 4 behavioral stories
  - 5 questions to ask them

- Do not add heavy new topics.
- Clean up wording and make answers shorter.

## Interview Day

- Keep answers structured.
- Say what you personally owned.
- Mention measurement and validation.
- Bridge codec gaps honestly.
- Ask team-specific questions.

## After HM Interview

Immediately write notes:

- Questions they asked
- Topics they cared about
- Any weak answers
- Names / team details they shared
- Panel topics they hinted at

Then update the technical-panel prep around those signals.
