# Questions To Ask The Hiring Managers

Pick 3-5 depending on time. Prioritize technical and team-success questions.

## Best Questions

1. What are the most important technical problems this team is solving right now across PlayStation SDKs and cloud gaming?

2. For this role, where do you expect the biggest performance challenges: codec latency, GPU/CPU synchronization, memory bandwidth, hardware integration, or streaming quality?

3. How much of the work is new feature development versus debugging, profiling, and optimization of existing codec pipelines?

4. What does strong performance in the first 6 months look like?

5. How does the team validate quality and latency regressions across hardware, SDK, and cloud gaming environments?

6. How closely does this team collaborate with platform, hardware, and system software groups?

## If Speaking With Seema Kataria

Use manager-oriented questions:

- What traits make someone successful on your team?
- How do you divide ownership between individual engineers and cross-team efforts?
- What are the biggest ramp-up challenges for someone joining this team?

## If Speaking With Jason Wang

Use senior-manager / scope questions:

- How does this team fit into the broader PlayStation SDK and cloud gaming strategy?
- What technical areas are becoming more important for the team over the next year?
- Are there platform or hardware constraints that strongly shape the engineering roadmap?

## If They Ask "Any Concerns About The Role?"

Good answer:

> No major concerns. The area I would ramp hardest on is the codec-standard-specific side. My plan would be to pair that with my existing strengths in C++, GPU programming, profiling, and debugging so I can contribute while deepening codec expertise.

## Closing Question

If time remains, ask:

> Based on our conversation, is there any area where you would want me to go deeper or clarify my experience?

This gives you a chance to correct a concern before the interview ends.
